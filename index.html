I am a PhD candidate in Computer Science at the Australian National University, specialising in combinatorial optimisation, constraint programming, and branch-and-bound algorithms. My doctoral research centres on the maximum common subgraph (MCS) problem — developing novel upper bounds, symmetry-breaking frameworks, and heuristic-exact hybrid methods to push the frontier of exact graph matching.

I bring three years of production software engineering experience at WSO2, where I built core components of the Ballerina programming language. I am currently seeking postdoctoral opportunities in combinatorial optimisation and constraint programming.
